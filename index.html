☞ About This Archive ☜

Colorado's frontier ghost stories are usually treated as quaint folklore — colorful local color from the gold-rush era. This archive treats them differently. Read alongside scholarly work on settler colonialism, mining labor, and the politics of haunting, these stories reveal themselves as a working component of the ideology that made westward expansion possible. They are not neutral. They confess violence and conceal it in the same breath.

Browse by category. Read carefully. The dead are paying attention.
